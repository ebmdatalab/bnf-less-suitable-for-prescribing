Indications and dose

Bacterial infection in otitis externa

To the ear

  • For Child

    (consult product literature).

  • For Adult

    (consult product literature).

Bacterial skin infections

To the skin

  • For Child

    Apply up to 3 times a day, for short-term use only.

  • For Adult

    Apply up to 3 times a day, for short-term use only.

Bowel sterilisation before surgery

By mouth

  • For Adult

    1 g every 1 hour for 4 hours, then 1 g every 4 hours for 2–3 days.

Hepatic coma

By mouth

  • For Adult

    Up to 4 g daily in divided doses usually for 5–7 days.

Unlicensed use

With topical use in children

Neomycin Cream BPC—no information available.

Contra-indications

When used by ear

patent grommet (although may be used by specialists, see Ear); perforated tympanic membrane (although may be used by specialists, see Ear)

With oral use

intestinal obstruction (in adults); myasthenia gravis (aminoglycosides may impair neuromuscular transmission) (in adults)

With topical use

neonates

Cautions

When used by ear

avoid prolonged use (in children)

With oral use or when used by ear

avoid prolonged use (in adults)

Cautions, further information

With oral use in adults

Although neomycin is associated with the same cautions as other aminoglycosides it is generally considered too toxic for systemic use.

Large areas

With topical use in adults

If large areas of skin are being treated ototoxicity may be a hazard, particularly in the elderly, and in those with renal impairment.

Large areas

With topical use in children

If large areas of skin are being treated ototoxicity may be a hazard in children, particularly in those with renal impairment.

Interactions

Route-specific information

Since systemic absorption can follow topical application, the possibility of interactions should be borne in mind.

Individual interactants:

  • Neomycin

Side-effects

Frequency not known

When used by ear

local reaction

With oral use

blood disorder (in adults); confusion (in adults); diarrhoea (in adults); drug cross-reactivity (in adults); electrolyte imbalance (in adults); gastrointestinal disorders (in adults); haemolytic anaemia (in adults); nausea (in adults); nephrotoxicity (in adults); nystagmus (in adults); oral disorders (in adults); ototoxicity (in adults); paraesthesia (in adults); superinfection (in adults); vomiting (in adults)

With topical use

sensitisation (cross sensitivity with other aminoglycosides may occur)

Side-effects, further information

Although neomycin is associated with the same side effects as other aminoglycosides, it is poorly absorbed after oral administration.

Pregnancy

With oral use in adults

There is a risk of auditory or vestibular nerve damage in the infant when aminoglycosides are used in the second and third trimesters of pregnancy.

Hepatic impairment

With oral use in adults

Manufacturer advises caution (increased risk of ototoxicity and nephrotoxicity).

Renal impairment

With oral use in adults

Avoid–risk of ototoxicity and nephrotoxicity.

With topical use

Ototoxicity may be a hazard if large areas of skin are treated.

Monitoring requirements

Monitoring of patient parameters

With oral use in adults

Renal function should be assessed before starting an aminoglycoside and during treatment.

Auditory and vestibular function should also be monitored during treatment.

Less suitable for prescribing

With topical use

Neomycin sulfate cream is less suitable for prescribing.
