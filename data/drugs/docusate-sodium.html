Indications and dose

Chronic constipation

By mouth

  • For Child 6 months–1 year

    12.5 mg 3 times a day, adjusted according to response, use paediatric oral solution.

  • For Child 2–11 years

    12.5–25 mg 3 times a day, adjusted according to response, use paediatric oral solution.

  • For Child 12–17 years

    Up to 500 mg daily in divided doses, adjusted according to response.

  • For Adult

    Up to 500 mg daily in divided doses, adjusted according to response.

By rectum

  • For Adult

    120 mg for 1 dose.

Adjunct in abdominal radiological procedures

By mouth

  • For Adult

    400 mg, to be administered with barium meal.

By rectum

  • For Adult

    120 mg for 1 dose.

Removal of ear wax

To the ear

  • For Adult

    (consult product literature).

Pharmacokinetics

With oral use or rectal use

Oral preparations act within 1–2 days; response to rectal administration usually occurs within 20 minutes.

Unlicensed use

With oral use in children

Adult oral solution and capsules not licensed for use in children under 12 years.

Contra-indications

With oral use

avoid in intestinal obstruction

With rectal use

avoid in intestinal obstruction

Cautions

With oral use or rectal use

do not give with liquid paraffin; excessive use of stimulant laxatives can cause diarrhoea and related effects such as hypokalaemia

With rectal use

rectal preparations not indicated if haemorrhoids or anal fissure

Interactions

Route-specific information

Interactions do not generally apply to topical use of docusate unless specified.

Side-effects

Rare or very rare

With oral use

abdominal cramps; nausea; rash

Frequency not known

When used by ear

skin reactions

Pregnancy

With oral use or rectal use

Not known to be harmful—manufacturer advises caution.

Breast feeding

With oral use

Manufacturer advises caution—present in milk following oral administration.

With rectal use

Rectal administration not known to be harmful.

Directions for administration

With oral use in children

For administration by mouth, solution may be mixed with milk or squash.

Less suitable for prescribing

When used by ear

Ear drops less suitable for prescribing.

Medicinal forms

There can be variation in the licensing of different medicines containing the same drug.

Capsule, Oral solution, Ear drops, Enema
